Inspiration

Anyone with a toddler at home has felt the need for a helping hand at some point. So sit back, relax and let the baby sitter do it's job. The baby sitter skill for Amazon Alexa aims to educate and entertain toddlers, via engaging kid friendly content. With the built in randomness, no two interactions with the babysitter will be identical, in an attempt to prevent boredom. With the interactive stories, rhymes, counting game, color game and more, the babysitter strives to provide engaging content for toddlers.

What it does

The baby sitter provides:

  • Interactive rhymes
  • Interactive story
  • Counting, Animal sounds and color games

How I built it

Using Node.js and the Alexa SDK as the platform, I used behavior3.com's BehaviorTree Editor & the Javascript BehaviorTree implementation, to easily manage conversations.

Challenges I ran into

Managing text used in engaging conversations is one of the hardest part of building conversational bots. BehaviorTrees (behavior3.com) provides a decent editor to handle text and state management that was leveraged to create the Baby Sitter.

Accomplishments that I'm proud of

Integrating Behavior Trees to provide easy maintainability/extensibility of conversations, leading to fresh content for kids.

What I learned

Behavior Trees, the Alexa platform & Node.js

What's next for Alexa, launch the baby sitter

Improve the (Number of Sessions) / (Number of unique customers) ratio to > 5 over a period of a month. (Currently at 1.2) Update interactions based on user feedback More Interactive Rhymes, Stories and engaging content
