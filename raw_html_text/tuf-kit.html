• Early Iteration of TUF Kit (internals)

Inspiration

Over 1,000 days of operating as a communications engineer in war zones around the world gave me an amazing idea:

How about we, as a startup, build a system to solve the communications problems every single person in joint special operations are having on a daily basis?

We set out to do just that, and created a Task Force.

TF Ninja!

What it does

The TUF Kit equips the warfighter with powerful facial-recognition software in a ridiculously tiny package while creating your own network on the move.

An onboard deep neural-network (DNN) and a infrared camera using GPU-accelerated architecture provides trainable computer-vision. An SSD with encrypted data-at-rest provides secure recording. Our proprietary breadcrumb networking allows for last-mile connectivity and low-bandwidth live-streaming.

The TUF Kit solves the communication gaps that currently plague forward-deployed infrastructure, in an affordable solution that thrives on modularity, adaptability, and ease of deployment.

How we built it

We brainstormed the challenge over the past two weeks when Off The Grid was announced. Size, weight, bandwidth, and universal-utility, were the biggest challenges going into this.

Using commercial parts (COTS), mostly open-source software, and some unique patent-pending designs and utilities as of Friday May 17th, we set out to execute the idea this weekend to hack our solutions.

Challenges we ran into

Correct amperage for power. Cooling/heat dissipation. Proper software. Missing pin-connectors, headers - little things that impeded progress.

3D printing had lack of adhesion to build-plate, leading to warping. Redesign of enclosure to to improve durability, reduce size, spark joy.

Proper paperwork submission / organization. Cognitive overload stifled decision-making processes.

Accomplishments that we’re proud of

We’re finally building something people actually want!
