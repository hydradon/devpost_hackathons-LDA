Inspiration

The main inspiration came from foreign countries where they've tried to solve a problem of having unsatisfied citizens for whom was really hard to communicate to their town hall / government. Another inspiration was Czech website https://www.zmente.to/ that we found not effective (Prague only, only complaints — no citizen participation, bad feedback, bad marketing, old-fashioned website).

What it does

It is a portal for public suggestions and complaints about the city improvement. Others can vote (upvote/downvote system) for complaints they consider important and relevant and offer a help.

How we built it

From a scratch, building our own idea, doing public research, creating user-flows, wireframes and high-fidelity prototypes. After that we implemented our own CMS and front-end.

Challenges we ran into

Creating our own CMS was harder than we thought at the beginning aaand small amount of time of course :slightly_smiling_face:

Accomplishments that we're proud of

We created a really useful website that is possible to use in all Czech cities to improve local development. Also we made in human-centered, so that the users don't have problems with using it.

What we learned

Solving public/town related things is really not easy and proposed solutions have many caveats. Also, we are pretty good at web dev for newbies.
