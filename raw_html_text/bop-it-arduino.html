Inspiration

As a child, we loved playing Bop It! However, it is socially unacceptable for anyone over the age of 10 to play regular Bop It!, so we created a more "sophisticated" version.

Also, we just wanted to learn Arduino.

What it does

Users can "Cover It!" using a photoresistor, "Press It!" with a button, and "Flip it!" with a switch. A light will flash corresponding to the action the gamer needs to take, and if the user makes the correct move then a confirmation LED will flash, indicating the user's sucess.

How I built it

We used the Arduino Uno starter kit and multiple sensors. We looked at Arduino tutorials online to understand how to wire the breadboard and code the program.

Challenges I ran into

The main challenges were my sleep-deprivation and lack of knowledge about hardware and software, but we were able to overcome this using online resources and the collaboration among our team.
