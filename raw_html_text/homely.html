Where did the idea come from?

The idea for Homely came with the need to be notified of who is at the door before you actually have to open it. Instead, we wanted a smart home doorbell that can do much more using facial recognition software and IOT.

What does it do?

Homely recognizes a face and turns on led lights depending on the direction that a person turns their head.

How I built it

Homely was built using openCV and premade har cascades for facial recognition.

Challenges I ran into

We ran into issues with the openCV software and trying to put it on a portable device like the raspberry pi. Eventually, when we were able to get openCV on the raspberry pi the facial recognition was not working as well.

Accomplishments that I'm proud of

I am proud that we were able to persevere and continue to experiment with facial recognition despite the technical difficulties. We worked well together as a team and created a product that has a lot of potential.

What I learned

We learned about facial recognition and how to optimize it to recognize not just your face but also movements in certain directions. We also were able to learn more about the raspberrypi and how to make the two compatible.

What's next for Homely

With more time we hope to make Homely more compatible with the home by connecting it to Amazon Aws IOT and having some sort of web application or app that can tell you the status of the home.
