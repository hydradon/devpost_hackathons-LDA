• Why we do what we do
  • 12% of Singaporean residents suffer a mental illness during their lifetime
  • First onset of mental illness tends to occur in young adulthood (20-26)
  • Professional mental health treatment is stigmatized and avoided
  • Only 30% seek professional help, with average delays from 4 to 13 years 
  • Lack of social support because majority of people are unwilling to disclose their mental illness to friends and family
  • Among 50,000 patients, general practitioners only correctly identified depression in 47.3% of cases

=> Problems:

  • Early stage support is not sought by individuals experiencing mental illness
  • Mutual sharing and empathy are what individuals are more likely to seek prior to any professional counselling services
  • Not enough trustworthy resources for these individuals to approach promptly and conveniently
  • Disconnect between individuals and professional organizations in their efforts to tackle mental illness
