Inspiration

Awareness of socially stigmatized issues made us realize that people suffering from these conditions often face isolation and feel like they are alone dealing with these problems.

What it does

The first purpose of our site is that it provides a safe-space for people suffering from these stigmatized issues where they can read about similar experiences. The second purpose is that it provides a platform for users to share their heartwarming messages that ultimately lets other users know they are not alone.

How we built it

We created a fullstack website hosted on Google Firebase. To do this, we implemented a Javascript/Node.js server, hosted on Google Cloud Functions, to manage REST calls between our front-end site and our Google Realtime Database. We also implemented a simple UI design for our website with Javascript, jQuery, HTML, and CSS.

Challenges we ran into

At first, we had trouble getting started and implementing the base of our project, Firebase. It took us a while to understand how to use REST APIs to talk to our front-end and our database.

Accomplishments that we're proud of

We are proud that we finished our project and implemented a fullstack functioning website, that works as we had intended it to. It was great to be involved in creating a webservice from the ground-up.

What we learned

We learned, first and foremost, a great amount about web-services, REST APIs, and serving dynamic data. We also, however, learned more about these marginalized people who suffer from stigmatized illnesses/conditions. Doing this hackathon has given us a new and important perspective to consider.

What's next for You are not alone!

We will definitely continue to polish and fine-tune our service, adding more details and features.
