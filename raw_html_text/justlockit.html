How we built it

We built our own quasi-servers/database. We had a lot of trouble with Azure and other services since none of us are CS majors (2 Mechanical Engineers and 2 Electrical Engineers), so we just decided to bypass all of that and make our own, which is something we are definitely very proud of. Everything is mostly handled by socket programming in Python, and the database management is done with Java.

Challenges we ran into

Running the DragonBoard headless was definitely very tricky, especially given that Debian is a lot trickier than Ubuntu. Also, socket programming got pretty nasty at times. All connections were reliable, but early testing often required a full power cycle to kill

Accomplishments that we're proud of

We bypassed entire servers and built a (very scaled-down) DIY version!

What we learned

We learned the difficulty of merging different languages. We all did not have the same knowledge coming in, but we understood that and did our best to work with what we had.
