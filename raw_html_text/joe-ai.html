Inspiration

People have loved playing around with the advances AI has brought to images, making your friends look old or putting them in a Van Goth painting - but no one has made it easy for people to use the recent advances in natural language processing to create their own AI's to write, generate and converse from textual data.

How would your friend speak as an AI? Could you get an AI to rewrite the last episode of Game of Thrones? Which celebrity would you most like to speak with?

Once off the ground, this can be extended into developing conversational AI for videogames, helping draft documents just like you would and more - democratizing access to NLP.

This isn't only for fun, there are important ethical considerations. Many don't appreciate the high level of text and voice cloning that is available, making them vulnerable to scams or fake news. By playing with the current state of the art, we will give people insight and understanding into it's power and limitations, and soothe anxieties about advancing technology.

What it does

Uses transcripts to understand a celebrity, friend or document - and then generate new text and audio like them. As a centrepiece, we cloned Joe Rogan - so that you can be a guest on his podcast today.

How I built it

We are leveraged machine learning models pretrained on millions of text documents (such as all of wikipedia and the news form the last 5 years) and fine tuned them on podcast transcripts and audio for our purposes - just as users will be able to do. We used the state-of-the-art ML models GPT-2 and DC-TTS for text and audio respectively.

Challenges I ran into

A model is only as good as the data you give it. We ran into large challenges in training the audio model, and in integrating the various components. Because the models are standalone and take up significant GPU memory, voice and text generation can't run at the same time. In future, we would fix this with batching user requests on separate cloud machines.

Accomplishments that I'm proud of

Getting the text generation model to generate incredibly realistic conversations. This was a careful balance between training the model to imitate Joe's personality and not overtraining it to the extent that it lost the ability to generalise.

What I learned

How to sensitively balance training regimes on ML models, integrate ML models with a website for production. The often surprising things that an AI will say!

What's next for Joe.AI

Productionising what we used to train Joe Rogan into an easy to use product so people can use their conversations with friends or other data with our models. Then see what consumers use the product for. We have many potential applications to explore such as:

  • Generating scripts and audio for video game characters drastically reducing production costs.
  • Draft messages, improving productivity.
  • Generating voices for figures in wax museums like US Presidents! -Rewriting the final episode of Game of Thrones.
