Use Case

One in 68 U.S. children has an autism spectrum disorder (ASD), a 30% increase from 1 in 88 two years ago, according to a new report released Thursday by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. There is a growing demand for affordable and safe education providers for families with children diagnosed with ASD.

Object Recognition

Programmed lessons are catered to child's needs in order to enhance cognitive learning capabilities. Pepper is able to create interactive lessons and make it fun for any child to learn and engage with technology.

Demonstrating Consistency

Instructional strategies are applied and implemented in the same way on a regular basis. This helps the child predict what will happen next and increases the probability of their success.

Reward Good Behavior

The one on one interaction provides a two-way conversation and allows Pepper to engage with the child by rewarding them with natural gestures and actions. Pepper loves to dance!
