Inspiration

After experiencing many occasions where either my parents, friends, or I are faced with tons of receipts, valuable documents, and other papers and are unsure what to do. With Doc-Scan, a Data Management App, we are now able to simply title the document, scan it (picture), and download to our phones instead of digging through tons of documents. As a matter of fact, one member of our team, Sasank Aduri, was denied access to a SoHacks5 computer for we did not have proper identification with us. This is actually where the hearth of our idea sparked.

What it does

DocScan is an Andriod app which takes a picture(scan) of the document at hand and: 1) Names the Document, Date and Time 2) Saves it to the Android Device 3) Organizes it by time scanned

Once the document is scanned, there will be an option to share the scan with messages, whatsapp, hangouts, gmail, yahoo, google photos, google plus, etc.

How we built it

We built DocScan by using MIT App Inventor 2 since it was a platform that many of us were familiar with, was more available to us at the time, seemed straightforward and hassle-free, and we could not attend Android App Development APP class. We are using a AMAZON BLU Android Phone for this app.
