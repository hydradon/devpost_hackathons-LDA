Inspiration

A large majority of NPO concentrated in the health care service and research area having an astounding charity efficiency level of only a C+ . This means, that for every $100 spent, only $60-70 (sometimes lower or higher) is actually getting to the cause.

NPOs are continuing to seek more and more capital, but there is little evidence indicating that "just simply giving more" is necessarily going to create bigger change faster in the world. To the contrary, one should be weary of the effects that influxes of money have had in the past on organizations. In the for profit world, more money generally means hiring more people, assembling more committees and producing slower results and with an aggressively tedious bureaucratic processes.

On the other extreme, capital constraints in the world of technology and venture capital have surprisingly continued to breed unicorn status level companies in record time.

So who's right, and how does this help charities?

What it does

Aurora is a Charity exploration and Performance Escrow service. It allows would be donors to find their ideal charities, honestly assess their execution level, and make decisions about whether or not to join in funding the cause.

The Performance Escrow concept is the key, in that like a traditional escrow account funds are not released until both party's fulfill their duties to the arrangement. Aurora's performance escrows act much like Bountry contracts as they incentivize a certain behaviour by restricting a pool of cash until a set objectives or expected target efficiency is reached. At which point, charities are free to redeem funds from the PerformanceEscrow contract and build their cause.

How I built it

Aurora is built using Ethereum, and Solidity, using the Truffle DApp framework with use of React, Redux, and UPort.
