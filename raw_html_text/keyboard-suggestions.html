RGB Keyboards are lit, but not enough

What's wrong: We're using them as entertainment when they can actually increase productivity

Clearly the first goal of having an RGB keyboard is to have a thicc battle station, and thus in general RGB keyboards are aimed especially at the gaming crowd. But this is a missed opportunity for making the experience of learning how to use a computer more interactive: Young and elderly alike are more eager to write faster on the keyboard and not type using only their index fingers

Finger guides

For people currently getting accustomed to the keyboard typing experience, having a keyboard that indicates good writing posture by outlining each typing zone for fingers could be a fantastic aid

Concept: Letter suggestions

Taking it a step further we could enable suggestions and prediction of words for slow learners, and allow the expansion of vocabulary.

Error correction

The changing of color of the keyboard to red when making an error while typing, or in any environment could increase the interaction and thus retain-ability of the situation, making people less prone to repeating the mistake

Expanding the horizon

The ability to make the keyboard reactive to the environment of the PC could enable the user to become more productive when using the keyboard. For example, highlighting the ctrl + ... options Pressing ctrl will open up the menu of possible commands depending on the application
