Problem

Working as an architect across many departments Sam experienced trouble when it comes to communication with colleagues from other departments working together on the same project and with coworkers from external companies involved in a specific phase of the project. Site architect, the one that physically walks around the construction site, runs frequent check-ups and detects issues or any situation that has to be reported to further internal departments (e.g. interior design dept.) and to external companies in charge for that part (e.g. electrical engineering company). This process is usually very time consuming for all the parties and not efficient at all.

Apart from the time consumption, to describe the problem they spend from 20min to 1hour over phone, and take photos that are usually being sent via email, whatsapp or slack. Communication would be much more precise if they used videos, but those would than have to be stored and organised well. All the media files, such as plans, CAD drawings, photos and even videos are usually stored in different folders on desktop computers or sincronised via dropbox. Everytime architect need to access a specific file he has to look it up among dozens of folders and different versions, which also takes time. Not to mention that not all of them have the latest version of plan syncronised on their devices and plans can be updated daily.

User scenario

Sam is a site architect and detects an issue with implementation of the lighting on the 2nd floor od 100-floor skyscraper in Dubai due to incompatibility with electricity adapters. First he calls his fellow interior designer Lucas, who developed the lighting concept and explains the issue over phone in high detail. He may take photo and send him over email or slack. Afterwards, Lucas delegates the task to his colleague who calls the other company in charge for all electrical installations. This call is also time consuming and afterwards, his remark is awaiting to be processed. After several hours or next day the company contacts their on-site workers to check out the issue. After they fix the issue they update the interior design department who removes this task from their pipeline and site architect checks the site again wether the problem has been solved. If neccessary, site plan is updated with new changes and now all the parties have to have the latest version of the plan.

Solution

In order to cut off the uneccessary waste of time of all of the parties involved, and to make their intersecting workflows more efficient, they can use Arthur, a collaboration cross-platform app that enables project management team and associated architects to upload of all project documentation including site plans and detailed plans. Each plan can be marked with current updates and issues to be examined and resolved. All internal and external parties can collaborate by directly communicating about parts of the project they are involved in. This way each party doesn't need a mediator nor to wait for days until the issue is addressed by third party. Each party is instantly notified on relevant issue. Each marking is described by tags, date, status and appropriate content (photos and videos), with the ability to live stream the position of particular problematic area during the conversation.

Arthur is syncronised with the media destination portal of the architectural company (build on top of MediaSpace platform). There could be found all company's media files and project documentation for each project. Such platform makes their content organised and enables collaboration with many contributors, moderators and viewers in a multitude of channels, projects and departments. All their contend is safely stored and site is to authorized to company employees but some channels are made public to gain visibility or to be accessible by other external contributors.
