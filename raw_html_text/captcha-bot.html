I have taken hundred's of Confident CAPTCHAs and I find them to be very annoying. I wondered if they were actually effective or just a waste of time.

If you give this bot an image, it can tell you whether or not it is a store front.

I built it using Google Cloud's AutoML vision.

I had to dfind hundreds of images of storefronts and hundreds of images of Google street view images which don't have store fronts to train the computer.

The average precision is .998, which means that this AI is now more accurate than a human at solving these CAPTCHAs

Computers can be trained to solve Confident CAPTCHAs faster and more accurately than humans. Therefore, we should use other forms of CAPTCHAs instead of subjecting humans to meaningless Confident CAPTCHAs.
