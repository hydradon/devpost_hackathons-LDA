Inspiration

After listening to the presentation about Metro's phone plans and add-ons, I could tell that the same speech is given at least ten times a day in each of the thousands of Metro stores across the nation. So I decided to come up with a way to scale up this presentation in a consistent and enjoyable way to new customers.

What it does

Metro assistant is a virtual assistant that guides customers in deciding the right phone plan for them. This is all done in a very natural setting that puts customers at ease.

How I built it

I built Metro Assistant using a series of 3D animation tools that I had never used before. I also had to code in c# to get certain aspects of the Assistant working.

Challenges I ran into

Being new to 3D animations, I was hit with a huge learning curve that I was not expecting. Despite this, I persevered and surprisingly enjoyed the journey it took me on.

Accomplishments that I'm proud of

I'm proud of the fact that this concept will not only benefit Metro but a lot of industries. I was afraid of not having a good concept but once I came up with Metro Assistant, I knew I was onto something big.

What I learned

Perseverance and hard work can get you anywhere

What's next for Metro Assistant

I will continue to improve on this concept and hope to see it bear fruit in the near future
