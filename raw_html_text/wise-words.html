Inspiration

Even the best optimist and lightest heart can use a spark of motivation to ignite the soul fire to face the day.

What it does

Some mornings it can be a little (or a lot) challenging to find the motivation needed to face the coming day. The "Wise Words" of some of the worlds great minds might help you.

Need a reminder that the world holds great promise but it's up to you to get up, to sally forth, to seize it? "I went to the woods because I wished to live deliberately, to front only the essential facts of life, and see if I could not learn what it had to teach, and not, when I came to die, discover that I had not lived." Henry David Thoreau

Need a reason to keep a smile on your face and song in your heart regardless of the obstacles in your path? "No pessimist ever discovered the secret of the stars, or sailed to an uncharted land, or opened a new doorway for the human spirit." Helen Keller

How about just a bit of mirth and merriment to brush away the melancholy of cloudy day? "Don't sweat the petty things and don't pet the sweaty things." George Carlin

Wise Words has the medicine to sooth the soul and soar the spirit.

Just say, "Alexa, ask Wise Words for a quote." or just "Alexa, start Wise Words."

After Wise Words answers with a random quote, it will prompt, "Another quote?" You may say, "Yes", "No", or any of the following: "Repeat that" if you want to hear the same quote again. "Thumbs up" if you heard a quote you particularly liked.
Once you have a few favorites, just say, "Alexa, ask Wise Words for one of my favorite quotes." or just "Favorite quote" after the prompt.

Other things to say: "Alexa, ask Wise Words for quote 127" or, after the prompt, say "Quote 127" or just "127." "Alexa, ask Wise Words to clear my favorites" or, after the prompt, say "Clear favorites."

So, whether you need some extra pep in your step, a boost to your bounce, a helping hand with your heart, or a panacea for your pain; look to the "Wise Words" of the world's great minds for your cure.
