Inspiration

Our project originated as a way for teachers to have extra eyes in the classroom to help them recognize which students are paying attention and which are not. This evolved into the idea of providing the teacher with live feedback during their lessons to show how the students are feeling in the class. Rather than having students fill out check boxes on an evaluation form at the end of the semester, our project will read students' emotions and provide the teachers with more accurate data in real time to improve their lectures on the fly.

What it does

The project uses Microsoft's cognitive services and emotion API to read students' emotions while they are sitting in the classroom. The lecturer will be able to create an online classroom where students can join to begin sending their feedback to the teacher. Students are also able to anonymously submit any questions to the classroom instead of having to raise their hand.

How we built it
