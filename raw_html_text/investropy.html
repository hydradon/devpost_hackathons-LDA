Inspiration

Investropy was born from the idea of wanting students to exercise their passion for activism while also gaining financial literacy.

As students ourselves, we realized that many of our peers have little to no knowledge about the stock market, but often have an extensive knowledge on the causes they are passionate about and will go out of their way to do further research on these topics.

We also realized that many large corporations donate to charities and partake in social activism, often using this as a part of their marketing strategy. Examples of this include the Coca-Cola corporation donating to World Wildlife Fund to help polar bears, the Bell corporation launching the BellLetsTalk campaign, and much more. This practice serves as a bridge between finance and charity work.

By investing in the stocks of these corporations, young adults are able to indirectly donate their money to the charities they care about while also having an opportunity to make this money back as the stock prices rise. A win-win situation!

What it does

Investropy is an informative web application that serves to help young adults make educated decisions on their future stock investments based on the causes they are passionate about.

Previously conducted studies show that 85% of today’s youth consider themselves to be an activist. By engaging in protests and discussions both online and offline, it’s safe to say that this generation has no shortage of people who aren’t afraid to stand up for what they believe in.

However, there are many young adults who want to make a difference in this world but might not have sufficient funds to make donations or the time to volunteer. Investropy works to encourage these young adults to take an alternative way of getting involved.

Now, how does this all work?

Major corporations such as Google, Microsoft, and others all donate a portion of their net earnings towards charitable organizations. By investing in these companies’ stocks, the user is able to indirectly donate to these charities through their investments and participation in the stock market. Therefore, not only is the user putting their money towards a good cause, but they also have the opportunity to make that money back through their stocks and further develop their financial literacy beyond its basics (ie. budgeting, taxes, etc).

How we built it

We first drafted high fidelity wireframes on Adobe XD to plan our web application, allowing us to have a visual guide through this process. We then custom made all graphics and icons through the sketching software Clip Studio Paint. After this, we started building the database with MySQL and then started working with Brackets to create the HTML and CSS files of the website. We then used JavaScript, chart.js, docker, php, and bootstrap.

Challenges we ran into

We had originally wanted to use Alpha Vantage to implement a real time stock API, however, none of us were familiar with this API and were quite short on time, so we decided to build a database containing all the stock information of companies we wish to present, and have specific data be extracted from there using mySQL instead.
