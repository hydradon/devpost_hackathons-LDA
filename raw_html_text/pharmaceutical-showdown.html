Inspiration

I wanted to learn more about the pharmaceutical and healthcare industries while creating a game that would be fun for others to play and learn with me. I wanted to solve the problem of people's lack of understanding of the pharmaceutical industry.

What it does

This game allows for someone to form their own startup to attempt to compete with pharmaceutical giants in the industry. You race to earn one thousand dollars before the other corporations, while learning about the field in-between each round. You have to react to the news about the industry presented each round and react accordingly with your price setting. You can only control your price as the CEO; you have to react to your production and other company's prices.

How I built it

My program was developed completely in Java with the use of Java Swing and AWT classes, along with a helper class to keep track of each business's statistics. Most of the reactions to the market is based on a random number generator inside of the Random class inside of the Java library. These reactions are based on the price of other company's and how much is being sold within the company. The news in-between each round creates multipliers that change the ability for prices, production, and stability to change.

Challenges I ran into

I used a terrible program for GUI design (Java Swing), that lacks any sort of graphics engine or reliable text editor. This major challenge pressed me to explore the opportunities I had within these constraints, and forced me to adapt to a new coding environment. Furthermore, I had no prior knowledge about the pharmaceutical industry, so I had to commit myself to extensive research on the topic before creation of this game.

Accomplishments that I'm proud of

I learned a multitude of information about the pharmaceutical industry and created a realistic market that reacts to your decisions and random occasions. Each company appears to have a "mind of its own" and doesn't follow any other company in specific ways. I also made Java Swing not look like Windows 95.

What I learned

Through my work I discovered what makes the pharmaceutical industry change and how they operate. Furthermore, I learned about the process of drug creation and the history of the pharmaceutical field. Lastly, I established an understanding of the major corporations in the pharmaceutical industry and figured out how to create a game within Java Swing elements.

What's next for Pharmaceutical Showdown

The next steps for this game would be expansion upon the number of businesses involved, more individualization of your company, greater control over production of your drugs, a graphics engine, and several more songs on the soundtrack.
