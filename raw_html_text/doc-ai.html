Inspiration

1 in 20 people is misdiagnosed each year in the United States based on the data from medicalnewstoday. That is equivalent to 12 million people. Misdiagnosis can be threatful to human life. We want to change that.

What it does

Doc.AI is a tool for doctors that will assist doctors in diagnosing various diseases by scanning X-Rays and MRI scans with an iPhone and/or iPad. We are starting with four diseases: Brain Tumors, Pneumonia, Tuberculosis, and Scoliosis

Due to the simple UI, Doc.AI is easy to use:

  1. Choose the disease
  2. Point the camera at the X-Ray or an MRI scan
  3. Tap the shutter button
  4. Get the prediction

That is not all!

World-known doctors will be able to become a Doc.AI contributor.

We want to help doctors’ better diagnose and provide proper treatment for their patients, and to do that we need doctors’ to help us diagnose those patients.

Through a leaderboard-led scoring system, qualified doctors can help train our models by answering simple questions about our prediction, in case it was incorrect. They will also be able to compete against each other by earning DocPoints each time their contribution is accurate.

Lastly, once Doc.AI gives a prediction, the doctor can send the prediction information to the patient's primary physician for further treatment

How we built it

The app was built using Swift and CoreML framework to classify images. In addition, we have trained four models (for Brain Tumors, Pneumonia, Tuberculosis, and Scoliosis).

Challenges we ran into

Training your own model sounds easier than it is. This process is very time-consuming. We had FOUR. Due to this event being only 24-hours, we had to work in a hurry to get as much training data as possible. The rest of the time was spent on making the UI pretty and testing the models.
