How to play "Super Word"

Go to Alexa Skill Testing Tool , click and hold the microphone button or hold down the space bar on your keyboard to activate the microphone and say "Open super word". Wait for the response, and then say "Start a quiz" to begin playing.

Inspiration

Speech and language delays and disorders affect 1 in 12 children in the United States [3] and are considered the “most common disability of childhood, least diagnosed by primary care physicians.” [4] Furthermore, delays in early identification of these conditions can result in delays in treatment, which is crucial for children to develop communication and literacy skills for school readiness as well as behavioral and mental health.

It is estimated that smart speakers (e.g., Amazon Echo and Google Home) are already in 30 million American homes, and they are used regularly by children and are designed with features specifically for younger users [2]. However, none of the existing voice-enable interaction on these smart speakers is capable of detecting and assessing children's early speech development, despite its ubiquitous computing power for automatic speech recognition.

What it does

"Super Word" is an Alexa quiz game for children age 4+ (as well as adults who are non-native English speakers) to play in a quiet environment independently. This game provides a sound effect, a question, and or a brief description for the child/adult to guess words that cover most phonemes in English [1] across multiple categories (e.g., nouns, actions, and adjectives). By using the Amazon automatic speech recognition to process the child's pronunciation during the gameplay experience, "Super Word" provides a preliminary screening of an individual’s speech production in order to determine whether the child’s speech development is on track or whether a non-native speaker's speech is intelligible. This information can be extremely crucial for both parents and healthcare providers (e.g., pediatricians) as well as educators.

How we built it

We first identified our user group and a list of vocabulary content from prior research, and then wrote the scripts and added associated sound effects from the Amazon Sound Library . By following tutorials on Alexa Skills Kit , and modifying a sample quiz program provided by the tutorials we integrated the front-end voice user interface with the back-end Amazon Lambda service.

Challenges we ran into

  • This was the first time anyone on our team tried working with the alexa developer console or javascript.
  • One of our team-mates was out for exam most of the second day, but our team caught up by the end.
  • It was difficult to connect the device and integrate front-end & back-end.
  • QA testing took longer than anticipated, required a dedicated quiet environment, and only one computer was able to connect to the Alexa Skill Testing Tool .

Accomplishments that we're proud of

  • Debugging together and supporting the design and development with each other.
  • Successful integration of Amazon Lambda with the front-end voice user interface.

What we learned

  • Explore all available assets in the library carefully first (e.g., sound library) and define the correct genre of game (e.g., quiz game vs. trivia game).
  • Ensuring device compatibility and network stability is crucially important for designing for IoT.
  • Teamwork makes the dream work and everyone can code and/or contribute.

What's next for Super Word

  • We plan to keep working on it and hopefully conduct some user testing with children/non-English speaking adults.
  • We plan to add additional functionality so that there is more than one correct answer that can be accepted.
  • More sound effects will be added to make questions easier to understand and provide hints.
  • Improve random number generator so that no questions are repeated during a single game.
  • Add different modes based on vocabulary subject.
  • We plan to submit a final prototype to the Student Research Competition at an upcoming conference.
