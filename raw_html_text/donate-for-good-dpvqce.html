The most common way of donating your old clothing and miscellaneous items from your house is to simply drop it off at a large thrift store such as the Goodwill or the Salvation Army. In the end, you think the items you donated are sold to somebody who can make a good use out of it. Not quite, the reality is that donated goods often take a much longer journey before it ends up either getting it resold or ending up the landfill. It’s more likely the ladder. We can not only prevent this from happening but also make a huge impact in people’s lives by giving these things directly to those in need by finding a specific organization and donation center that align with your cause. Donating to a women’s shelter or a local charity that aids women and children’s health can be far more gratifying.
