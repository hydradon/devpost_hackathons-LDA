Inspiration

We were worried about the safety of pedestrians, cyclists, and other members of the community that exercise in busy areas while being distracted by music. Our team members have had personal experiences or have witnessed cases where, for instance, a cyclist would be listening to music and not be aware of a car nearby and be vulnerable to danger.

What it does

Our sunglasses have a built-in camera in the frame that takes pictures with the press of a button (also on the frame). They also have bone conduction technology that lets you listen to music through vibrations in your head's external bone structure, instead of blocking your eardrums and thus surrounding noise.

How I built it

Our team used Solidworks to design the whole glass frame in order to integrate all the features and provide the best experience of bone conduction technology to our customers. The design files were printed by 3D printers and we assembled them together. Then, we built the amplification circuits with the bone conduction transducers, and tested the bone conduction transducers with several trials to ensure proper function. The mini-cameras were connected with to an SD card to save the images taken, bluetooth (wireless capabilities), and a button (activation).

Challenges I ran into

We ran into complications with trying to get the bone conduction transducers to work. The noise levels were not as strong in the beginning, so we had to connect them to an amplifier to increase the volume. We also ran into challenges with creating the whole product in general because there is not a lot of information online that we can learn from, so we had to talk to people in person and gather information ourselves from trial and error and other's experiences.
