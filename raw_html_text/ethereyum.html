Inspiration

Taking one smart contract and pay multiple parties/wallets.

What it does

Allows a transaction to be split into percentage amounts set in the smart contract amongst the parties being paid. For an example, a customer pays for an item, that transaction is then split between the seller of the item and the supplier who provided the goods/service to create the item (i.e. a coffee shop and its supplier of coffee beans).

The user initiates a transaction which contains the total amount value. They also specify a duration of time the contract lasts for, and what percentage of the total amount will be sent to different wallets and presses a button to make it run. This then allows the users that contain the other wallets to accept or ignore (cancel?) the transaction. If all members accept, the exchange happens.
