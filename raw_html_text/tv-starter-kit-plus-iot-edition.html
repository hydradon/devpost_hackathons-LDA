Inspiration

Hours of watching excellent television and hours of being frustrated by glare & eye fatigue!

What it does

Wirelessly controls bias lighting and the tilt of your TV from a smartphone app through bluetooth using an Arduino 101. Adding a bias light can reduce eye fatigue by making the TV less of a harsh target to focus on (especially at night). The tilt of your TV can effectively cut out glare.

The kit features an Arduino 101, a grove base shield, grove led module, and a servo. Communication between the app and kit is done through the built-in Bluetooth capabilities of the 101. The app's interface feature a slider that controls servo angle and a button to toggle the bias light.

How we built it

Arduino 101 with Anaren Atmosphere and the Arduino IDE

Challenges we ran into

Troubleshooting PWM for servo actuation Bluetooth communication

Accomplishments that we're proud of

The easy to use cross-platform app that seamlessly controls the system. We are also very happy with the effectiveness of cutting glare by just adjusting the TV angle.

What we learned

Wireless communication takes patience and diligence
