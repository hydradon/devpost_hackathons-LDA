Inspiration

Its a mixture of natural reviews coupled with free advertising for companies that worked hard to accommodate the disabled.

What it does

The Website currently allows companies to answer a 1 question where afterwards they label what services their company location provides and the name of the location.

How I built it

Coded each page in html5, used java-script to catch and gather data, used google Firebase to store important data to grab for later use.

Challenges I ran into

First time learning HTML, java-script, and Firebase. Free domains assume my HTML websites are phishing,yet not worth buying my own domain

Accomplishments that I'm proud of

Have a working website and database that interact to make a useful product. Database can gather data and website can call data from database and sort it

What I learned

How to use HTML, Javascript, and Firebase to create a website that can dynamically store and recall information

What's next for JPMorgan Chase Information on Accessibility

Getting a domain, Companies use a free sign in to more easily manage there accessibility statistics, and users choosing to browse as guest or make login. Free login users with "proven" track record leaving popular reviews can start helping the community by removing location names that do not meet requirement for being disabled accessible.
